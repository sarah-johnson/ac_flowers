How it works

If you have a random white flower on your island, you can't tell its genes. Depending on those underlying genes, it may or may not be capable of producing a given target color. If you observe the children of a breeding pair, you can make inferences about the underlying genetics of the parent flowers. This calculator uses Bayes' Theorem to make those predictions. Given a parent color, we assume to start with equal chances that flower could have any underlying genes. This isn't a perfect assumption, since the real distribution of flowers on your island is weighted based on seeds or natural flowers you started with. But the nice thing about Bayes's theorem is that our choice of "priors" matters less and less the more observations we get.

You can use this tool to track the probable genetics of flowers in your town.
